ECG Monitoring System

The ECG Monitoring System records the electrical activity of the heart over time. It is used to detect arrhythmias, monitor heart health, and assist in diagnosing cardiac conditions. This portable device is widely used in hospitals and home care. With Bluetooth connectivity, it enables remote monitoring, making it ideal for telemedicine applications.

Wireless EMG Monitoring

The Wireless EMG Monitoring device measures muscle activity wirelessly. It is widely used in rehabilitation, sports science, and clinical research. This system provides real-time data on muscle performance and fatigue, helping athletes and patients improve their physical health. Its compact design ensures mobility and ease of use during monitoring sessions.

EEG Monitoring System

The EEG Monitoring System records brain activity for neurological studies. It is used to diagnose epilepsy, sleep disorders, and brain injuries. This non-invasive device provides high-resolution data for precise analysis. Lightweight and comfortable, it ensures patient convenience during long monitoring sessions, making it a preferred choice in clinical settings.

Defibrillator

The Defibrillator is a life-saving device that delivers an electric shock to the heart to restore normal rhythm during cardiac arrest. It is essential in emergency care and is designed for ease of use. Modern defibrillators include voice prompts and visual instructions, making them accessible even to non-medical personnel in critical situations.

Cardiac Output Monitor

The Cardiac Output Monitor measures the volume of blood pumped by the heart per minute. It is crucial for assessing heart function in critical care and surgical settings. This non-invasive device provides real-time monitoring, helping clinicians make informed decisions during complex medical procedures. Its accuracy and reliability make it a vital tool in intensive care units.

PPG Sensor Project

This project involves the use of a Photoplethysmography (PPG) sensor to measure heart rate and blood oxygen levels. The PPG sensor detects changes in blood volume in the microvascular bed of tissue using light absorption. An Arduino Uno microcontroller is used to process the data and display the results on an LCD screen. The project demonstrates the integration of hardware and software to create a functional biomedical device. It is designed to be portable and cost-effective, making it suitable for remote health monitoring. The system is calibrated to ensure accurate readings and minimize noise interference. Applications include fitness tracking, telemedicine, and early detection of cardiovascular issues. The project also explores the potential for real-time data transmission via Bluetooth for remote monitoring. This hands-on project highlights the importance of biomedical engineering in improving healthcare accessibility. It serves as a foundation for developing more advanced wearable health monitoring systems.
